## Capability 1: Deployment Planning
1. Articulate how and where Claroty CTD should be deployed 
    1. Disconnected topologies (SiteA disconnected from SiteB)
    1. Data Aggregation (SiteA and SiteB report into unified view)

1. Articulate when, where, and how, to evaluate IT assets on the OT network.

1. Articulate data collection methods beyond network PCAP data/SPAN ports, and when to recommend each approach.

1. Given decisions on deployment topologies, articulate overall deployment technical requirements (hardware, network, throughput, storage, etc).


## Capability 2: Discovery & Inventory

### Scenario 2a: Discover and Classify Assets
1. Demonstrate the capability to discover and identify devices across OT and IT environments, regardless of protocol or physical connection (e.g. ``IP``, ``MODBUS``, or ``serial``). 

1. Include the ability to identify known, unknown, and transient devices.

### Scenario 2b: Boundary Definitions
1. Demonstrate the capability to classify and organize the asset inventory by network connectivity, Purdue Level, and role. Show how this inventory organization comprehensively identifies the boundaries of OT and IT networks so network-level policies and enforcements can be planned, implemented, and audited.

1. Map and classify networks by showing their physical and logical connections.

1. Clearly identify important devices and communication paths that represent the greatest risk.

### Scenario 2c: Network Segmentation / Separation
1. Demonstrate using Claroty CTD to design policies to enforce the secure segmentation of OT environments.
1. Demonstrate using Claroty CTD to monitor and alert on OT and IT protocols based on discovery and identification of insecure protocols, lack of encryption, etc (and how to align with CISA security guidance).
1. Demonstrate how Claroty CTD monitors connections across Purdue Levels and monitors and alerts on suspicious activity.
1. Highlight where controls cannot be enforced and require human processes and/or compensating controls.
