## Outcome 1: Deployment Planning
1. Articulate how and where Claroty CTD should be deployed 
    1. Disconnected topologies (SiteA disconnected from SiteB)
    1. Data Aggregation (SiteA and SiteB report into unified view)
    1. Methods to passively monitor access and core switch traffic

1. Articulate when, where, and how, to evaluate IT assets on the OT network (Windows, Linux, and Mac/OSX).

1. Articulate data collection methods beyond network PCAP data/SPAN ports, and when to recommend each approach.

1. Given decisions on deployment topologies, articulate overall deployment technical requirements (hardware, network, throughput, storage, etc).


## Outcome 2: Visibilty (Asset Discovery & Inventory)

### Outcome 2a: Discover and Classify Assets
With no prior knowledge of the environment, attendees are tasked with discovery and analysis of the Mission IT Lab. This environment has 20,000+ devices across dozens of geographically dispursed locations, spanning multiple networks and VLANs.

1. Demonstrate the capability to discover and identify devices across OT and IT environments, regardless of protocol or physical connection (e.g. ``IP``, ``MODBUS``, or ``serial``). 

1. Include the ability to identify known, unknown, and transient devices.

1. Serving as a cyber assessor, how do you generate a report showing what specific devices are on the network, what are they talking to, over what protocols, and are there any known vulnerabilities on the network?

1. If there are vulnerabilities on a particular network segment, what is the contextualized risk?


### Outcome 2b: Boundary Definitions
1. Demonstrate the capability to classify and organize the asset inventory by network connectivity, Purdue Level, and device role. Show how this inventory organization identifies the boundaries of OT and IT networks such that network-level policies and enforcements can be planned, implemented, and audited.

1. Map and classify networks by showing their physical and logical connections.

1. Clearly identify important devices and communication paths that represent the greatest risk.

### Outcome 2c: Network Segmentation / Separation
1. How do we use Claroty CTD to design policies to enforce the secure segmentation of OT environments?
1. Demonstrate using Claroty CTD to monitor and alert on OT and IT protocols based on discovery and identification of insecure protocols, lack of encryption, etc (and how to align with CISA security guidance).
1. Demonstrate how Claroty CTD monitors connections across Purdue Levels and monitors and alerts on suspicious activity.
1. Highlight where controls cannot be enforced and require human processes and/or compensating controls.
